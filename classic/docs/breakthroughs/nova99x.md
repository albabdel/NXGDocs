## See It in Action

NOVA99x eliminates false alarms and dramatically improves operational efficiency across GCXONE deployments:

### Scenario 1: Outdoor Perimeter Monitoring with Environmental Challenges

A distribution center has 80 cameras monitoring outdoor perimeters, loading docks, and parking areas. Before NOVA99x, operators received 400-500 alarms per shift triggered by: moving shadows from clouds, tree branches swaying in wind, vehicles passing on adjacent streets, insects near cameras at night, and rain/snow. Operators spent 4-5 hours per shift investigating these false positives, causing alarm fatigue and missed real threats.

With NOVA99x:
1. The system administrator enables NOVA99x with 95% noise suppression threshold
2. AI analyzes each motion event using multiple factors: object shape, movement pattern, context, and historical data
3. Day 1 results: 487 total motion events detected → NOVA99x filters out 482 false alarms (tree shadows, insects, weather) → 5 real alarms pass through (actual person detections)
4. Operators now review only 5 verified alarms instead of 487 raw events per shift
5. Response time to real threats improves from 8 minutes (lost in noise) to 45 seconds (immediate visibility)

The measurable impact: Operators reclaim 4.5 hours per shift previously wasted on false positives. That time is reallocated to monitoring 220+ additional cameras at other sites. Alarm fatigue disappears, and the team's accuracy rate increases by 40% because they're focused on verified threats instead of drowning in noise.

### Scenario 2: Urban Retail Chain with High Foot Traffic

A retail security provider monitors 50 stores, each with 8-12 cameras. The contract requires monitoring "unauthorized access after hours" (stores close at 10 PM). However, cameras trigger on passing pedestrians, car headlights, delivery trucks making early morning drops, and cleaning crews (authorized but triggering alarms). This generates 60-80 false alarms per store per night = 3,000-4,000 alarms across the chain.

With NOVA99x:
- NOVA99x is configured with "After Hours Retail" AI profile that recognizes: Authorized cleaning crew uniforms, Expected delivery time windows (4-6 AM), Pedestrians outside the storefront (not entering)
- The AI filters alarms based on: Person inside vs. outside store, Authorized uniform detection, Time-of-day context, Door/window breach sensors
- False alarm rate drops from 3,500/night to 50/night (98.6% reduction)
- The 50 remaining alarms are genuine threats: attempted break-ins, unauthorized persons inside closed stores, actual security events

The transformation: The monitoring center previously required 3 full-time operators dedicated to this retail chain. After NOVA99x, 1 operator handles the entire chain plus 2 additional client accounts (50% increase in capacity). Monthly operational costs drop by €10,000 due to reduced headcount requirements, while service quality improves because real threats get immediate attention.

### Scenario 3: Critical Infrastructure with Zero Tolerance for Missed Threats

A power utility secures 100+ remote substations with AI-enhanced perimeter detection. The challenge: They cannot afford to miss any real security threat, but environmental false alarms (animals, weather, vegetation) were triggering 200+ alarms daily. Investigating every alarm required significant resources, but ignoring alarms could miss critical intrusions.

With NOVA99x:
1. NOVA99x is deployed with "Audit Mode" enabled initially - AI flags potential false alarms but operators still review everything
2. Over 30 days, operators validate NOVA99x decisions: 5,847 alarms filtered by AI → operators confirm 5,831 were correctly identified as false (99.7% accuracy)

4. NOVA99x now auto-archives verified false alarms while escalating all potential genuine threats with AI confidence scores

The result: The utility maintains zero-compromise security posture while reducing investigation workload by 97%. Audit-ready logs prove every decision with AI confidence scoring. Compliance is maintained, threats are caught faster, and the security team handles 50% more sites without additional hiring. ## ⚙️ How to Activate NOVA99x

This section explains how to enable and configure NOVA99x filtering in your GCXONE instance.

### Prerequisites

Before activating NOVA99x, ensure you have:
- [ ] Administrator access to GCXONE
- [ ] NOVA99x license enabled
- [ ] AI Analytics enabled on compatible cameras

### Activation Steps

#### Step 1: Access Analytics Settings
1. Log into GCXONE as an administrator.
2. Navigate to **AI Insights** → **NOVA99x Configuration**.
3. Toggle the **Enable Global Filtering** switch to ON.

#### Step 2: Define Filter Sensitivity
1. Select a site or camera group.
2. Adjust the **Noise Suppression** slider (Recommended: 95% start).
3. Review the AI confidence threshold for alarm passing.

#### Step 3: Configure Alarm Queues
1. Navigate to **Alarm Management** → **Queues**.
2. Enable "NOVA99x Pre-Filter" for the operator queues.
3. Define the action for filtered alarms (e.g., Auto-Archive or Log Only).

#### Step 4: Validate with Insights
1. Go to the **NOVA99x Insights** dashboard.
2. Monitor the "Processed Alarms" vs. "Filtered Out Alarms" graph.
3. Look for the "Alarms Passed" percentage (Target: ~1-5%).

#### Step 5: Assign to Operators
1. In **Access Management**, assign the "Cleaned Queue" to the Operator role.
2. Verify that operators only see alarms verified as real threats by the AI.
