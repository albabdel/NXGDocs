## See It in Action

Pulse View transforms surveillance footage into actionable timelapse insights across GCXONE deployments:

### Scenario 1: Construction Site Progress Documentation

A general contractor manages a 12-month commercial building project with 16 cameras capturing different construction zones. The client contract requires weekly progress reports with visual documentation. Previously, the site manager spent 3-4 hours per week: reviewing footage, capturing screenshots, creating a PowerPoint presentation, and writing narrative updates.

With Pulse View:
1. Every Friday at 4 PM, the site manager opens GCXONE and selects the 4 primary cameras (foundation, framing, facade, interior)
2. For each camera, they select "Last 7 Days" and choose "50x speed" playback
3. Pulse View generates four 30-second timelapse videos in under 10 seconds each
4. The videos show the entire week's progress: Monday's foundation pour, Tuesday-Thursday's framing work, Friday's inspections - all compressed into viewable highlights
5. The site manager downloads the MP4 files, emails them to the client with a 2-sentence summary

The measurable impact: Weekly reporting time drops from 4 hours to 10 minutes (96% time savings). The client gains visual proof of progress instead of just text reports, reducing disputes about project timeline. Over the 12-month project, this saves 200+ hours of administrative work valued at €8,000+. The contractor now offers "weekly timelapse updates" as a premium service add-on, generating €500/month in additional revenue per project.

### Scenario 2: Retail Foot Traffic Analysis and Marketing Content

A shopping mall operator uses security cameras to monitor 8 retail stores. The marketing department wants to create promotional videos showing "the energy and excitement" of the mall during the holiday shopping season. Hiring a professional videographer for a week would cost €6,000-8,000, and requires coordination with tenants, shoppers, and legal for privacy releases.

With Pulse View:
- The marketing manager accesses GCXONE and selects cameras overlooking the main atrium, food court, and entrance
- They generate timelapses for Black Friday (6 AM - 10 PM, 16 hours compressed to 90 seconds at 100x speed)
- The timelapse shows: Early morning crowds gathering, peak shopping rush at noon, evening shoppers, all in a dynamic visual story
- Privacy masking is applied automatically to blur individual faces while showing crowd patterns
- The 90-second timelapse is exported and used in social media campaigns, email newsletters, and lease presentations to prospective tenants

The transformation: What would have cost €6,000+ in production is generated in 3 minutes for zero additional cost beyond existing surveillance infrastructure. The mall uses this content to attract 3 new tenant leases, each worth €50,000+ annually. Pulse View transforms a security system into a marketing and business development tool, doubling the ROI of the camera investment.

### Scenario 3: Incident Investigation Context

A warehouse experiences a product damage incident - 12 pallets of goods found damaged on Tuesday morning. The warehouse manager needs to determine when and how the damage occurred. Reviewing 72 hours of continuous footage across 6 cameras would take 8-10 hours of manual scrubbing.

With Pulse View:
1. The investigator selects the 3 cameras covering the damaged area
2. Sets the time range: Friday 6 PM (last known good condition) to Tuesday 7 AM (damage discovered)
3. Generates timelapses at 100x speed - 72 hours compressed to 45 minutes of review time
4. Watching the timelapse reveals: Normal activity Friday, forklift operator error Saturday at 2:17 PM (pallet knocked), damage not reported, additional impacts Sunday morning
5. The investigator exports the critical 5-minute segment showing the initial incident for training and insurance documentation

The result: Investigation completes in 1 hour instead of a full workday. Root cause is identified with certainty (operator error + failure to report). The timelapse evidence is used for: retraining the forklift operator, insurance claim with exact timestamp, and process improvement (new damage reporting protocol). The warehouse saves €2,000 in investigation costs and recovers €15,000 from insurance because they have defensible timestamped evidence.

## ⚙️ How to Activate Pulse View

This section explains how to enable and generate timelapse videos using Pulse View.

### Prerequisites

Before activating Pulse View, ensure you have:
- [ ] Administrator or Manager access to GCXONE
- [ ] Pulse View license enabled (available via MarketPlace)
- [ ] Cameras with stable recording history

### Activation Steps

#### Step 1: Access MarketPlace
1. Log into GCXONE.
2. Navigate to **MarketPlace**.
3. Locate **Pulse View** and click **Activate**.
4. Assign the feature to the desired customers/sites.

#### Step 2: Select Your Camera
1. Go to **Live View** or **Site Map**.
2. Select the camera you wish to generate a timelapse for.
3. In the device menu, look for the **Timelapse** icon.

#### Step 3: Define Time Range
1. Click on the **Timelapse** icon.
2. Select the "From" and "To" dates in the calendar picker.
3. Choose the playback speed (e.g., 10x, 50x, 100x).

#### Step 4: Generate the Video
1. Click **Generate Pulse View**.
2. The AI will immediately begin stitching the frames from the existing surveillance feed.
3. The video will be ready for viewing in seconds.

#### Step 5: Download or Share
1. Click the **Download** icon to save the MP4 file.
2. Use the **Share Link** to send the timelapse directly to a client or stakeholder.
3. The video is archived in the site's **Media Library** for future reference.

### Best Practices
- **DO** choose camera angles that cover the entire area of interest.
- **DO** use the highest possible recording resolution for professional results.
- **DO** protect the privacy of bystanders by using the **Masking** tool before sharing.
- **DON'T** generate timelapses for time periods with massive signal loss.
- **DON'T** use timelapses as the sole source of legal evidence (always reference the raw footage).
