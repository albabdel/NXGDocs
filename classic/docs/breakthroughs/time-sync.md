### Scenario 1: Multi-Camera Incident Reconstruction

- The final report has handwritten timestamp corrections, making it less credible for legal proceedings
- Law enforcement requests clarification 3 times because the timeline is confusing

With Time Sync:
1. All 6 cameras maintain perfect synchronization via GCXONE Cloud Time (accurate to &lt;1 second)
2. The investigator exports footage from all 6 cameras for the incident window: 14:20-14:30
3. GCXONE automatically creates a synchronized multi-camera view with a unified timeline
4. The sequence is crystal clear: Camera 1 (14:23:15), Camera 3 (14:24:42), Camera 2 (14:25:18), Camera 4 (14:26:03), Camera 5 (14:27:29), Camera 6 (14:28:51)
5. The export includes a tamper-proof timestamp verification certificate

The measurable impact: Investigation time drops from 1 hour to 8 minutes (87% faster). The synchronized evidence is accepted without question by law enforcement and prosecutors. The suspect is convicted based on the clear timeline, and the retailer avoids a €25,000 loss dispute because the evidence is legally defensible.

## ⚙️ How to Activate Time Sync

This section explains how to enable and maintain perfectly synced timestamps across your infrastructure.

### Prerequisites

Before activating Time Sync, ensure you have:
- [ ] Administrator access to GCXONE
- [ ] Time Sync license enabled
- [ ] Connectivity to a reliable NTP (Network Time Protocol) server or GCXONE Cloud Time

### Activation Steps

#### Step 1: Access Device Configuration
1. Log into GCXONE.
2. Navigate to **Configuration** → **Device Management**.
3. Select a site or group of devices.

#### Step 2: Enable Time Sync
1. In the device settings, locate the **Time & Date** section.
2. Toggle the **Enable Time Sync** switch to ON.
3. If a device is out of sync (e.g., "45 min behind"), a notification will appear. Click **Sync Now** to align it instantly.

#### Step 3: Configure Time Sources
1. Go to **Settings** → **System** → **Time Services**.
2. Select **GCXONE Cloud Time** as the primary source for maximum accuracy.
3. Define a secondary local NTP server for redundancy.

#### Step 4: Validate Synchronization
1. Navigate to the **Insights** → **System Health** dashboard.
2. Look for the **Time Sync Accuracy** widget.
3. Verify that 100% of devices show "In Sync" status.

#### Step 5: Audit Log Protection
1. Enable **Timestamp Locking** in the security settings.
2. This ensures that once a frame is recorded, its timestamp cannot be modified, creating a legally defensible audit trail.
