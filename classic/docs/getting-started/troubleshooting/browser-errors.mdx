# 🍪 Resolving Browser Cookie Errors

If you can access the Genesis Dashboard but receive an **"Oops something went wrong"** message when clicking on **Alarm Management** or **Workflows**, the issue is almost certainly your browser's security settings.

## 🔍 The Root Cause
Talos is an **embedded application**. It runs inside the Genesis interface but communicates with a separate security backend. To maintain your session across these two domains, your browser must allow **third-party cookies**.

---

## 🛠️ How to Fix: Quick Toggle

The fastest way to fix this is using the address bar toggles:

1. Look at the right side of your **Browser Address Bar**.
2. Click the **Eye icon** (👁️) or **Cookie icon**.
3. Toggle the switch to **"Allow Third-Party Cookies"** for this site.
4. Refresh the page.

---

## ⚙️ Manual Browser Settings

If the quick toggle isn't visible, use the manual settings for your specific browser:

<Tabs defaultValue="chrome">
  <TabItem value="chrome" label="Google Chrome">
    1. Go to `Settings` → `Privacy and Security` → `Third-party cookies`.
    2. Select **Allow third-party cookies**.
    3. *Alternatively:* Add `[*.]nxgen.cloud` and `[*.]evalink.io` to the **"Allowed to use third-party cookies"** list.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="edge" label="Microsoft Edge">
    1. Go to `Settings` → `Cookies and site permissions`.
    2. Ensure **"Allow sites to save and read cookie data"** is On.
    3. Ensure **"Block third-party cookies"** is Off.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="safari" label="Safari">
    1. Go to `Settings` (or Preferences) → `Privacy`.
    2. **Uncheck** "Prevent cross-site tracking".
    3. **Uncheck** "Block all cookies".
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

---

## 💡 Troubleshooting Checklist

- [ ] **Incognito Mode:** If Talos works in Incognito, clear your regular browser cache.
- [ ] **Ad-Blockers:** Some ad-blockers (like uBlock Origin) treat Talos cookies as "trackers." Whitelist the site in your extension settings.
- [ ] **Corporate VPN:** Some VPNs strip cookie headers. Try connecting from a direct internet line to rule out network-level blocking.
