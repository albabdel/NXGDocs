# 📊 Bandwidth Requirements Reference

Proper bandwidth planning is essential for reliable video surveillance. This reference provides the formulas and metrics needed to calculate capacity for your GCXONE deployment.

## 📐 Calculation Formula

Use this formula to estimate the total bandwidth required for your installation:

<Callout type="info">
**Total Bandwidth =**  
`(Number of Cameras × Avg Bitrate)` +  
`(Concurrent Viewers × Streaming Bitrate)` +  
`30% Management Overhead`
</Callout>

## 📉 Bitrate Guidelines

Bandwidth consumption varies significantly based on resolution and the codec used. **H.265 (HEVC)** is highly recommended for 50% bandwidth savings.

## 🏗️ Installation Examples

### 🏠 Small (10 Cameras)
- **Resolution:** 1080p (H.265)
- **Upload Required:** ~30 Mbps
- **Viewers:** 2 Concurrent Streams
- **Total Recommended:** **50 Mbps Symmetric**

### 🏢 Medium (50 Cameras)
- **Resolution:** Mixed 1080p/4MP (H.265)
- **Upload Required:** ~200 Mbps
- **Viewers:** 10 Concurrent Streams
- **Total Recommended:** **400 Mbps Symmetric**

### 🏭 Large (200+ Cameras)
- **Resolution:** Mixed (H.265)
- **Upload Required:** ~1 Gbps
- **Total Recommended:** **2 Gbps Dedicated Link**

## 🚀 Optimization Strategies

- **Use H.265 Everywhere:** Ensure your cameras and NVRs are set to HEVC/H.265 to cut data costs.
- **Motion-Based Bitrate:** Configure **Variable Bitrate (VBR)** so the stream only spikes during actual activity.
- **Sub-Streaming:** Use a lower resolution "Sub-Stream" for multi-camera grid views, and only switch to "Main Stream" for full-screen inspection.
- **VLAN Segmentation:** Keep video traffic on a dedicated VLAN to prevent congestion with general office data.

---

## ❓ Common Issues

- **Freezing/Artifacts:** Usually caused by sustained upload congestion. Try reducing the frame rate from 30 fps to 15 fps.
- **Delayed Alarms:** Often a result of network saturation. Implement **Quality of Service (QoS)** to prioritize alarm handshake traffic (Port 443).
