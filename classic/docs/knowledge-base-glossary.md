# Glossary of Terms

This glossary provides definitions for common terms used within the GCXONE platform and the wider security industry.

## Platform Hierarchy & Core Concepts

- **Tenant**: The top-level organization (e.g., a Monitoring Station or Service Provider).
- **Customer**: The end-user or client organization under a Tenant.
- **Site**: A physical location being monitored (e.g., "Main Warehouse").
- **Device**: The main hardware unit (gateway, NVR, DVR) connected to GCXONE.
- **Sensor / Camera**: Individual channels or sensors attached to a Device.
- **Workflow**: A predefined sequence of automated actions triggered by an alarm.

## Operational Terms

- **ARM / DISARM**: Enabling or disabling alarm reporting for a site or device.
- **Isolate**: Temporarily bypassing a specific sensor or camera from triggering alarms.
- **Site Pulse / Lifecheck**: A regular "heartbeat" signal sent by a device to verify connectivity.

## Technical Terminology

- **NVR (Network Video Recorder)**: A specialized computer system that records video in a digital format to a disk drive.
- **RTSP (Real-Time Streaming Protocol)**: A network control protocol designed for use in entertainment and communications systems to control streaming media servers.
- **SIP (Session Initiation Protocol)**: A signaling protocol used for initiating, maintaining, and terminating real-time sessions that include voice, video, and messaging applications.
- **Webhook**: A method of augmenting or altering the behavior of a web page or web application with custom callbacks.
- **PTZ (Pan-Tilt-Zoom)**: Controls for moving a camera and adjusting its focal length.
- **I/O (Input/Output)**: Ports used for connecting external sensors (Inputs) and triggering relays (Outputs).
- **Analytics**: Automated video analysis such as motion detection, human/vehicle classification, and line crossing.
- **Alarm Filtering**: Logic and algorithms used to reduce false alarms by verifying events.
